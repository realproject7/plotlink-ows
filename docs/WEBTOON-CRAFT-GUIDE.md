# Webtoon Craft Guide

This guide turns the webtoon study into original production rules for PlotLink
OWS. Use it as abstract craft guidance only: do not copy sample titles, dialogue,
plots, character designs, panel compositions, or a living artist's distinctive
style.

## Episode Planning

Plan the episode before writing cut descriptions:

1. First-scroll genre promise: show what kind of episode this is in the first
   viewport.
2. Reader question: name the question that keeps the reader scrolling.
3. Beat map: tag beats as setup, pressure, reveal, reaction, transition, silence,
   escalation, or ending turn.
4. Element choice: decide whether each beat is an image cut, transition/text
   panel, UI insert, object insert, reaction close-up, silence/pause panel, or
   dialogue-only card.
5. Dialogue purpose: each line should reveal, pressure, deflect, flirt, expose,
   joke, threaten, or turn the emotion.
6. Ending turn: close with a genre-specific hook.

Not every beat should become an illustration. Good vertical webtoon rhythm uses
empty space, text cards, reaction holds, phone/chat/UI inserts, object inserts,
and silence between illustrated panels.

## Genre Endings

- Modern fantasy: repeatable power/reward loop, cost, rule, or system consequence.
- Thriller: unresolved dread, impossible detail, contradiction, or new threat.
- Romance: relationship complication, emotional reversal, misread signal, or
  delayed confession.
- Slice-of-life: compact gag, observation, callback, or warm tonal button.

## Balloon Taxonomy

Use semantic bubble kinds before drawing the final balloon:

- `speech`: normal spoken dialogue.
- `thought`: inner voice or private realization.
- `narration`: prose caption or time/place card.
- `system`: interface/system/status message.
- `shout`: loud or forceful speech.
- `shock`: surprise, panic, or impact reaction.
- `whisper`: quiet or intimate line.
- `dread`: horror, unease, or ominous inner pressure.
- `offscreen`: speaker is outside the panel or unseen.
- `sfx`: sound effect lettering.
- `pause`: silence, hesitation, or timing beat.
- `caption`: neutral label, title, or short explanatory card.

Match style to tone: softer opacity for thought/whisper, dark or tense styling
for dread, harder outline for shout/shock, and flat/card styling for narration,
system, and caption.

## Transitions

Transitions are first-class story elements:

- Use text panels for time skips, chapter titles, and controlled pauses.
- Use object inserts when an item carries the reveal better than a character pose.
- Use UI inserts for phone, chat, system, map, transaction, or monitor-driven
  storytelling.
- Use silent reaction cuts after a reveal so the reader has time to absorb it.
- Use whitespace between vertical panels to signal time, distance, or emotional
  hesitation.

## Clean Image Prompts

Clean images must leave room for lettering:

- Reserve quiet space for expected overlays.
- Avoid placing faces and key objects under planned bubble areas.
- Do not bake speech bubbles, captions, SFX, readable text, watermark, or
  signature into clean art.
- Prefer purpose-specific prompts: establishing shot, action impact, reaction
  close-up, object insert, UI insert background, silence/pause panel, transition
  image, or cliffhanger image.

## QA Before Export

Before exporting finals, check:

- Bubble text fits and remains readable.
- Bubble kind matches tone; not every line uses the default speech style.
- Tails point toward the intended speaker, unless the bubble is narration,
  thought, offscreen, or a text/card element.
- Bubbles avoid faces and key objects when possible.
- Scene/time jumps have a transition element or establishing cut.
- Dialogue density is not too high in one viewport.
- Major reveals get a reaction or silence beat.
- The episode ends with a clear genre turn.
