# The Blank Spot

The map shouldn't have been in Box 1471.

Mira knew every box in the sub-basement. She'd catalogued them herself over three years — 2,847 boxes of forgotten cartographic artifacts, each one logged, dated, cross-referenced. Box 1471 was supposed to contain Ordnance Survey drafts from the 1960s. Routine stuff. The kind of work that made her colleagues' eyes glaze over and made Mira feel like the world still had edges.

She almost missed it. A single sheet of heavy vellum, slipped between two survey maps like someone had hidden it there on purpose.

*That's not survey paper.*

She pulled it out carefully, angling it under her desk lamp. Hand-drawn. Ink, not print. The lines were precise — not the shaky work of an amateur but the confident strokes of someone who'd spent years with a ruling pen. Coastlines, elevation markers, depth soundings. All the hallmarks of professional cartography.

And at the center: an island.

---

Mira set the map flat on her light table and pulled up the coordinates on her laptop. 66.2N, 7.8W. North Atlantic. Between Iceland and the Norwegian coast.

Nothing.

She refreshed the satellite view. Zoomed in. Zoomed out. Switched to three different mapping services. Open ocean. Every single one showed open ocean.

*Okay.* She took a breath. *It's a draft. A rejected proposal. Someone's fantasy island that never made it past peer review.*

But the depth soundings were too specific. The elevation contours too detailed. This wasn't imagination — this was observation. Someone had *been there* and drawn what they saw.

She flipped the vellum over. No date. No attribution. No institutional stamp. Just a single notation in the bottom corner, penciled in handwriting so small she needed her loupe to read it:

**Do not digitize.**

---

"Hadley, I need you to look at something."

She called him from her car, parked in the university lot with the heater running. It was 11 PM. She'd meant to leave three hours ago.

"Mira." His voice was rough with sleep. "It's Thursday."

"I know what day it is."

"Thursday means I've been asleep since nine. What's wrong?"

"I found a map in the archive. Hand-drawn, professional quality. It shows a landmass at coordinates that correspond to open ocean. No date, no attribution. And someone wrote 'do not digitize' on the back."

Silence. Not the polite silence of someone waiting to respond. The heavy silence of someone choosing their next words very carefully.

"Hadley?"

"Where exactly did you find it?"

"Box 1471. It was filed between—"

"Don't." His voice had changed. Harder now. The warmth gone. "Don't tell me the box number on the phone."

*What?*

"Come to my office tomorrow. Bring the map. Don't photograph it, don't scan it, don't show it to anyone." A pause. "And Mira — check if the archive has cameras in the sub-basement."

He hung up.

She sat in her car, engine idling, the map in its acid-free sleeve on the passenger seat.

*He didn't ask what the island looked like. He didn't ask if I was sure.*

She stared at the phone in her hand.

*He already knew.*
