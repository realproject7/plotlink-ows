# The Last Cartographer

A map that shouldn't exist.
A landmass no satellite has ever seen.
And the one person trained to know the difference.

Mira Voss draws maps by hand in the basement of a university archive — the last cartographer in a world that stopped needing them. When she uncovers a hand-drawn chart depicting an island between Iceland and Norway, she assumes it's a forgery. The coordinates are precise. The topography is detailed. And no record of the island exists in any database on Earth.

But someone knows it's real. Within days the map is stolen, her archive broken into, and her former mentor — the only person she trusts — tells her to stop looking.

> She's spent her whole life mapping what exists. Now she has to find what someone erased.
